## Notes & open calls

1. **Card count drift.**
   The filter bar reads "All 22" and "Digital 16, Studios 6." The grid actually contains 23 cards (22 anchor cards + the hybrid placeholder slot, plus the lululemon slot-frame at the foot). When the lululemon card has art and ships properly, decide whether to (a) bump the filter count to "All 23 / Digital 17", (b) leave it as "22" if lululemon is parked, or (c) remove lululemon from the grid until art lands.

2. **Hybrid placeholder.**
   Card 12 is a non-anchor slot announcing the first hybrid case study. Its tag "Hybrid · In production" and the "Coming soon" micro-label are intentional placeholders, not retired copy. They go live when the first hybrid case study lands.

3. **Only one case study links to a real page.**
   Card 02 (TELUS & Koodo) links to `case-telus-koodo.html`. Every other card has `href="#"`. The CTA labels read as if cases are accessible ("Read the case →", "Watch the film →") — when a card's destination doesn't exist, the link should either route to a coming-soon state or use the same hybrid-placeholder pattern.

4. **Hover loops on three cards.**
   Cards 02 (TELUS & Koodo), 03 (BCWHF), and 04 (Chinatown BIA) have `data-video` attributes and matching MP4 posters. Other cards are static. As more cards get video loops, this should become the default treatment rather than an exception.

5. **Studios proof inside the grid.**
   Iron Mountain (card 01) and Ernest Packaging (card 09) both carry "Silver ADDY, 2026" inline in the blurb. That's a deliberate redundancy with the homepage proof spine — the receipts reach the reader twice. Don't remove without considering the proof system holistically.

6. **lululemon copy is thin.**
   "Digital experience work for one of Vancouver's signature brands" is brief and generic compared to the rest of the grid. Either replace with a more specific blurb when the case study is written, or hold the card back until then.
